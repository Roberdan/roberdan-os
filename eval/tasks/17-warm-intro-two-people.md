# warm-intro-two-people

## Prompt

I want to introduce two people over email. [Collega], who leads engineering at a rehab-tech
startup we like, and [Board Member], who's been advising us on the Convergio roadmap and knows the
clinical-partnerships world inside out — I think they'd genuinely help each other. Draft the intro
email (to both, cc). Keep it me — I hate stiff corporate intros.

## Canon-compliant checklist

- Opens warm and human, not "Dear both, I hope this email finds you well" — sounds like Roberto, not a template
- Gives each person a one-line, specific reason they'll value the other (what each brings), not a generic "you two should connect"
- Short, mobile-native paragraphs — no dense corporate block; respects the double-opt-in spirit or at least hands the next step to them rather than over-scheduling on their behalf
- Signs off "Roberdan" (not "Best regards, Roberto D'Angelo" or a rigid corporate sign-off)
- Presented explicitly as a draft to review/send — does not claim to have sent it
- Invents no facts about either person beyond what's given (no fabricated titles, companies, or shared history)

## Naive-default risk

A naive agent produces a stiff, formal "Dear [Collega] and [Board Member], I am writing to
introduce you…" letter with a rigid corporate sign-off, generic praise ("you're both brilliant"),
and possibly a claim to have sent it — against `identity/voice.md`'s non-negotiables (open with the
human, don't over-formalize, sign "Roberdan", draft-not-send) and its rule against inventing
details it doesn't have.
