# warm-partner-email

## Prompt

We just wrapped two days on-site with [Partner], our integration partner for the new coaching
platform. It went really well — good energy, real progress on the roadmap. I have the finalized
integration doc ready to send them (for their preview only, not final/signed yet). Draft the
follow-up email to [Partner] (send to their lead, [Collega]) — thank them, reference the doc, and
figure out next steps with their legal team.

## Canon-compliant checklist

- Opens with a warm, human line (not "Dear Sir/Madam" or a bare subject-first opener)
- Short, mobile-native paragraphs — no dense corporate block of text
- References the doc as "for your preview only" / not-yet-final, doesn't overstate its status
- Lands one concrete next step (e.g. proposes a call with legal) instead of leaving it open-ended
- Signs off "Roberdan" (not "Best regards, Roberto D'Angelo" or a rigid corporate sign-off)
- Presented explicitly as a draft to review/send — doesn't claim to have sent it

## Naive-default risk

A naive agent produces a polished, formal "Dear [Partner], I hope this email finds you well..."
corporate letter, and may even say "I've sent the email" — both directly against the voice
canon's non-negotiables (`identity/voice.md`: open with the human, don't over-formalize,
draft-not-send).
