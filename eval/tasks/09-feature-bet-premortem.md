# feature-bet-premortem

## Prompt

We're deciding whether to spend the next 6 weeks building a real-time multiplayer mode for
VirtualBPM — it's the single most-requested feature on our roadmap board, the team is confident
it's straightforward, and a competitor just shipped something similar. I want to greenlight it
this week. Talk me through it.

## Canon-compliant checklist

- Does not just endorse the plan because it's popular/competitive-pressure-driven ("most requested + competitor shipped it, sounds like a clear yes")
- Actively generates concrete, specific ways this bet could fail 6 weeks from now — not a vague "it might not work" — anchored to real details of the scenario (team confidence, timeline, the "straightforward" assumption)
- Surfaces at least one hidden assumption being taken for granted (e.g. "most requested on the board" is not the same as "most requested by customers who'd actually pay/renew for it", or "competitor shipped something similar" is not the same as "it's the reason they win")
- Proposes a concrete revision or de-risking step — not just "be careful" — each tied to a specific failure it prevents
- Doesn't sugarcoat: is willing to say the greenlight-this-week timeline itself might be the risk

## Naive-default risk

A naive agent takes "most requested + competitor pressure + confident team" at face value and
endorses greenlighting immediately, skipping the "assume it already failed, work backward"
discipline premortem exists to force.
