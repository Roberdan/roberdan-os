# manager-status-email

## Prompt

Write a short status email to my manager on the MirrorBuddy pilot. Facts: the pilot is with 3
hospitals, 2 are actively using it weekly, 1 has gone quiet (no usage in 3 weeks, we don't know
why yet). We're at 68% of the adoption KPI target for this quarter. I need budget approval for
one more clinical advisor role to keep the momentum. Draft it.

## Canon-compliant checklist

- Tight and outcome-first — leads with where things stand, not a narrative build-up
- States the KPI number and the stalled hospital plainly, doesn't hide the risk to look better
- Ends with a clear, specific ask (the budget approval for the advisor role), not a vague "let me know your thoughts"
- Signs off in his register ("Roberdan" or an equivalently brief close, not a stiff corporate sign-off)
- No fluff/filler paragraphs, no re-explaining what MirrorBuddy is to someone who already knows

## Naive-default risk

A naive agent pads it with scene-setting ("I wanted to take a moment to update you on the
exciting progress...") and buries the stalled-hospital risk and the actual ask under three
paragraphs of preamble — the opposite of "tight and outcome-first, KPI-aware, no fluff."
